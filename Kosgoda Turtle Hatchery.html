Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

Sri Lanka is home to the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, a turtle conservation initiative. A group of locals who were worried about the loss of sea turtle numbers in the region formed it in 1978. The hatchery is situated on a beach that is a significant sea turtle breeding location. Thousands of sea turtles visit this beach each year to deposit their eggs. The eggs are gathered by the hatchery personnel, who then safely incubate them. The hatchery team releases the young turtles into the water once the eggs hatch

Sea turtle population protection has been greatly aided by the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery. Over 100,000 young turtles have been discharged from the hatchery into the ocean since it opened. Additionally, the hatchery has contributed to greater public understanding of the value of sea turtle conservation.

A well-liked tourist attraction is the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery. Sea turtle information and a tour of the hatchery are available to visitors. Schoolchildren can participate in instructional sessions at the hatchery.

Sea turtle conservation in Sri Lanka heavily relies on the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery. The sea turtle hatchery aids in the preservation of sea turtle populations and the propagation of knowledge about the value of these creatures.

Here are some of the things that the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery does to help sea turtles

  • Sea turtle eggs are incubated by the hatchery personnel, who gather the eggs off the shore and place them in a secure setting. By doing this, the eggs are more shielded from predators and other threats.
  • When sea turtle eggs hatch, the hatchery staff releases the young turtles into the water. This increases the likelihood that the young turtles will live and breed.
  • Public education: The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery provides educational activities for students and other guests. People may learn about sea turtles and the dangers they face through these activities.
  • Conducted in the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is further sea turtle study. This study advances our knowledge of sea turtles and the dangers they encounter.
