Bentota Turtle Hatchery

All visitors to Bentota enjoy visiting the Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project. The picturesque golden sands that entice you to our island also provide numerous endangered turtle species, such the Olive Ridley and Leatherback turtles, an alluring breeding site. The project's objective is to preserve and safeguard the eggs against predators and other threats. To find out more about these beautiful and endangered marine creatures, go to the turtle hatchery in Bentota.

things that you can do at the Benthota Turtle Hatchery.

  • The hatchery offers educational sessions to guests of all ages who want to learn more about sea turtles. You may discover more about the many sea turtle species, their life cycles, and the dangers they are subject to.
  • View sea turtles up close: There are several sea turtles on show at the hatchery. You can see these incredible animals up close and discover more about their habits.
  • If you go to the hatchery during the turtles' nesting season, you might be able to assist in the release of the young turtles into the sea. This is an experience you won't soon forget.
  • Donate: The hatchery depends on donations to keep operating. Please think about contributing to this crucial conservation initiative if you are able to.
